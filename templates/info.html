Exponential Moving Average

Beginner

EMA

EMA gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive to new information than SMA. It's particularly useful for identifying trend changes early and setting dynamic support/resistance levels.

Key Applications:

  • • Trend direction identification
  • • Dynamic support and resistance
  • • Entry and exit signal generation
  • • Crossover strategies with multiple EMAs

Best Timeframes:

15m 1H 4H 1D

Relative Strength Index

Beginner

RSI

RSI measures the speed and change of price movements to identify overbought or oversold conditions. Values above 70 suggest overbought conditions, while below 30 suggest oversold conditions.

Trading Signals:

Overbought (>70)

Consider selling

Oversold (<30)

Consider buying

Advanced Strategies:

  • • Divergence analysis
  • • Hidden divergence patterns
  • • RSI trendline breaks

MACD

Intermediate

Moving Average Convergence Divergence

MACD shows the relationship between two EMAs and is used to identify potential buy/sell signals when the MACD line crosses the signal line. It's excellent for trend confirmation and momentum analysis.

Components:

  • • MACD Line: 12-period EMA - 26-period EMA
  • • Signal Line: 9-period EMA of MACD line
  • • Histogram: MACD line - Signal line

Trading Signals:

  • • Bullish crossover (MACD > Signal)
  • • Bearish crossover (MACD < Signal)
  • • Zero line crossovers
  • • Divergence patterns

Average True Range

Intermediate

ATR

ATR measures market volatility by averaging the range between high and low prices over a period. Higher ATR indicates increased volatility, which is crucial for position sizing and stop-loss placement.

Practical Applications:

  • • Position sizing based on volatility
  • • Stop-loss placement (1-2x ATR)
  • • Profit target setting
  • • Market condition assessment

Volatility Levels:

Low Volatility ATR < 1% of price
Medium Volatility ATR 1-3% of price
High Volatility ATR > 3% of price
