Technical Analysis Indicators

Exponential Moving Average (EMA)

EMA gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive to new information than SMA. It's used to identify trends and potential support/resistance levels.

Simple Moving Average (SMA)

SMA calculates the average price over a specific period. It smooths out price data to identify the direction of a trend.

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

RSI measures the speed and change of price movements to identify overbought or oversold conditions. Values above 70 suggest overbought, while below 30 suggest oversold.

Average True Range (ATR)

ATR measures market volatility by averaging the range between high and low prices over a period. Higher ATR indicates increased volatility.

Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

MACD shows the relationship between two EMAs. It's used to identify potential buy/sell signals when the MACD line crosses the signal line.

Signal Indicators

Signal indicators provide buy or sell signals based on various technical analysis methods. They help traders make informed decisions about entering or exiting trades.

Volatility Indicators

Volatility indicators measure the degree of price fluctuation. They help traders understand market risk and adjust their trading strategies accordingly.
